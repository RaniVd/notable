Notable Announcements for Your Team in Slack

A quick way to get important messages to your team

How It Works

Type /notable in your chat command line, and it’ll hashtag your message as notable, and post it in the #General room. You can do this from any channel in Slack.

At the end of the day, you’ll also get emailed all the #notable announcements from the day. If you add additional hashtags, like #newfeature, your announcements will get grouped by these hashtags in the email:

Why the Harvest Team Uses Notable Announcements in Slack

Our Slack channels are fairly active at Harvest. GIFs are flying everywhere, people are asking what’s for lunch at all times of the day, and cryptic, random conversations are taking place in the #emoji-only channel. But, mixed in among all the madness, there are actually valid, important things that everyone in the company should know, like:

  • Hey team, we shipped this new feature today. It’s out to 75% of customers.
  • We’re hiring! If you know any developers, have them apply here.
  • Eike and his wife had a baby boy!

Isn’t this what email is for? Not quite. Emails are a bit stuffy and take time to put together. Slack, on the other hand, is much more informal and a quicker way to get a message out. That’s why we’ve chosen it as our channel to make important, company-wide announcements.

Installing Notable Announcements for Your Team

The Apps You'll Need
  • Slack account
  • Heroku account (free)
  • Mandrill account
Create A New App in Heroku
Getting Your API Keys from Slack
  • Login to your Slack account and go to Integrations.
  • Scoll to the bottom of the page to the DIY Integrations & Customizations section and click View on Slash Commands.

  • Enter /notable in the Choose a Command field and click Add Slash Command Integration.
  • Copy the Token provided on the next page and copy it into the SLACK_TOKEN field of the Heroku form. Leave this Slack page open.

  • In a new Slack window, go to the Slack Web API page.
  • Down in the Authentication section, click Create Token for the Slack account which you would like to use Notable Announcements.

  • Copy that token into the SLACK_API_TOKEN field in Heroku.
Getting Your API Keys from Mandrill
  • Next, login to your Mandrill account and go to Settings.
  • Click New API Key.
  • Create the API key and copy it into the MANDRILL_API_KEY field of Heroku.

    • Final Steps in Heroku
    • Fill in the remaining fields of the Heroku form. Enter the email you want the daily digests to get sent to in the TO_EMAIL field. Provide a TO_NAME for the email. Similarly, enter in the email address you would like the email to be sent from and the name of that–FROM_EMAIL and FROM_NAME.
    • Click Deploy for Free in Heroku.
    • To setup the daily emails, click Heroku Scheduler. Click Add new job and indicate the name of the job, and frequency and time you would like the email to be sent at.
    • Lastly, take the URL of your app from under Settings in Heroku and copy this into the URL field of your Slack Slash Commands page. If your app name is heroku-notable-test in Heroku, then your URL will be https://heroku-notable-test.herokuapp.com/notes.

    • Fill out the rest of the Slash Commands page fields in Slack as you wish and click Save Integration.
    • That's it! You're ready to starting using /notable in Slack!

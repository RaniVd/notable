Notable Announcements for Your Team in Slack

A quick, informal way to get important messages to your team

How It Works

Type /notable in your chat command line, and it’ll hashtag your message as notable, and post it in the #General room. You can do this from any channel in Slack.

At the end of the day, you’ll also get emailed all the #notable announcements from the day:

Why the Harvest Team Uses Notable Announcements in Slack

Our Slack channels are fairly active here at Harvest. GIFs are flying everywhere, people are asking what’s for lunch at all times of the day, and cryptic, random conversations are taking place in the #emoji-only channel. But, mixed in among all the madness, there are actually valid, important things that everyone in the company should know, like:

  • Hey team, we shipped this new feature today. It’s out to 75% of customers.
  • We’re hiring! If you know any developers, have them apply here.
  • Eike and his wife had a baby boy!

Isn’t this what email is for? Not quite. Emails are a bit stuffy and take time to put together. Slack, on the other hand, is much more informal and a quicker way to get a message out. That’s why we’ve chosen it as our channel to make important, company-wide announcements.

Installing Notable Announcements for Your Team

  • Create a new app in Heroku. Don't have an Heroku account? Signup for free.
  • Login to your Slack account. The new few steps will go through how to pull the two tokens you need from Slack.
  • Go to Integrations in Slack. Scoll to the bottom of the page and click View on Slash Commands.
  • Enter /notable in the Choose a Command field and click Add Slash Command Integration.
  • Copy the Token provided on the next page and copy it into the SLACK_TOKEN** field of the Heroku form. Leave this Slack page open.
  • In a new Slack window, go to the Slack Web API page.
  • Down in the Authentication section, click Create Token for the Slack account which you would like to use /notable.
  • Copy that token into the SLACK_API_TOKEN field in Heroku.
  • MANDRILL_API_KEY
  • Fill in the remaining fields of the Heroku form. Enter the email you want the daily digests to get sent to in the TO_EMAIL field. Provide a TO_NAME for the email. Similarly enter in the email address you would like the email to be sent from and the name of that–FROM_EMAIL and FROM_NAME.
  • Click Deploy for Free.
  • Take the URL you receive in Heroku and copy this into the URL field of your Slack Slash Commands page.
  • Fill out of the rest of the Slash Commands page fields as you wish and click Save Integration.
